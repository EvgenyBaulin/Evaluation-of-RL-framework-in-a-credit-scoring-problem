# Reference Study

## Core mechanics recovered from the reference project

- Environment interaction is weekly.
- The original state is the previous week acceptance rate.
- The original action is a discrete acceptance threshold from 5 to 100 with step 5.
- The simulation explicitly delays credit outcomes and weekly rewards.
- Reward is portfolio profit generated by accepted loans after defaults, repayments, and late recoveries materialize.
- The reference environment also supports separate thresholds for new and repeat clients even though the main paper example emphasizes a shared threshold.
- Distortions are modeled through shifts in score distributions, default rates, late-payment rates, and application volumes.
- The original experimental protocol uses a warm-up phase, an interactive training phase, and delayed-learning weeks that allow rewards from recent loans to arrive.

## What is reproduced in the new project

- Weekly threshold control instead of per-application approval decisions.
- Delayed credit outcomes and delayed reward realization.
- Simulation-based training and testing.
- Scenario-based robustness evaluation under distribution shift.
- Baseline comparison against RL controllers.

## What is extended in the new project

- Explicit split-policy control for new and repeat clients throughout the main setup.
- A modern config-driven experiment runner with quick and full profiles.
- A controlled state-dimensionality experiment that compares 12D, 20D, 30D, and 50D observations while holding the rest of the protocol fixed.
- Constraint-aware reward shaping with default-rate, approval-rate, capital-usage, and volatility penalties.
- Multi-agent comparison across DQN, Double-DQN, PPO, and SAC.
- Unified bootstrap confidence intervals for tables and time-series curves.
- Structured ablations and a writer handoff for thesis assembly.
